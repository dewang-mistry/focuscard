How it works

1

Pick one thing

Your iOS Reminders, grouped by list. Pick the one task you want to focus on.

2

See it big

Tap the task. The screen turns black, the title fills it, everything else disappears.

3

Keep it in sight

The screen stays awake. The task lives on your Lock Screen and Dynamic Island while you work.

4

Tap when done

Mark Complete checks it off in Reminders. Tap anywhere else to pause and pick a different task.

Why this works

FocusCard isn't a Pomodoro timer or a habit tracker. It's a digital index card — propped where the thing you most want to ignore lives. The research is well-established:

  • Attention residue Switching tasks leaves cognitive sludge that hurts your performance on the next one. A single visible focus reduces it. (Leroy, 2009)
  • The Zeigarnik effect Unfinished tasks loop in your mind until you externalize them. Putting the task on a card stops the loop.
  • Cognitive offloading Writing a task down measurably frees working memory — your brain stops tracking it because the card is. (Risko & Gilbert, 2016)
