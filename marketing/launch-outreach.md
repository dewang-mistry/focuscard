# Launch outreach — friends, family, colleagues

Three artifacts to use the moment Apple approves FocusCard:

1. Short text (iMessage / WhatsApp / SMS / Slack DM)
2. Email (longer, for colleagues + family)
3. Review prompt to paste into ChatGPT or Claude

**Don't send until the App Store listing is live.** The `focuscard.app` landing page works, but the App Store download link won't lead anywhere until Apple approves. After approval:
1. Confirm the listing is live (search "FocusCard" on iPhone)
2. Update the landing page download link if needed
3. Send in waves — closest 5-10 first (most reliable downloaders), broader list 24h later

---

## 1. Short text

```
I shipped my first iPhone app today — FocusCard. It puts the one
thing you're trying to focus on full-screen on your phone (and Lock
Screen) until you're done. Free, no ads, no tracking.

If you have a minute, downloading + leaving a quick review on the
App Store would help me a lot — reviews in the first few days are
weighted heavily by Apple. https://focuscard.app
```

## 2. Email

**Subject**: I shipped my first iPhone app — would love your help

```
Hey [name],

shipped something with my name on it: FocusCard.

It's a tiny iOS app that puts the one thing you're working on, huge,
on your screen and Lock Screen, until you're done. Built for the

three apps deep.

It's free. No ads. No tracking. The whole app runs on your device.
Made by one person (me) and one AI (Claude Code).

Two things would help me a lot if you have a few minutes:

  1. Download it: https://focuscard.app
  2. Leave a review on the App Store, even a short one

Reviews in the first few days are weighted heavily by Apple's
algorithm, so they matter way more than they would later.

If you don't know what to write — that's fine. Use the prompt below
in ChatGPT or Claude and it'll spit out something natural you can
edit and post. Takes 30 seconds.

Thank you, genuinely.

— Dewang
```

---

## 3. Review prompt

Paste this into ChatGPT or Claude:

```
Write a 40-60 word App Store review for an iOS app called FocusCard.

Context: FocusCard takes one task from your iOS Reminders and
displays it full-screen on a black background, with a matching
Live Activity on the Lock Screen and Dynamic Island. It's free,
on-device, no ads, no tracking. Built by an indie developer
shipping his first app.

I want the review to:
- Sound like a real human, not promotional copy
- Mention one specific feature I find useful (pick from: huge
  full-screen task, Lock Screen Live Activity, swipe to mark
  done, color-coded Reminders lists, no-distraction design)
- End with a star rating recommendation (4 or 5 stars)

Don't use words like "elegant," "essential," "seamless," "elevate,"
or "leverage." No paired em dashes. Plain language.
```

---

## Why this works

- **Short text** — fits in one message, no scrolling, clear ask, link.
- **Email** — leads with a specific, vulnerable opener ("first app with my name on it") so it doesn't read as a marketing blast. Names the AI partnership honestly. Two-item ask. Ends with the review prompt so you remove the friction of "ugh what do I say."
- **Review prompt** — turns "I should write a review" into a 30-second copy-paste-tweak. The negative-keyword list ("don't use elegant, leverage, etc.") keeps the output from sounding obviously AI-written.

## Sequencing

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Approval day | Send short text to 5-10 closest people (family, best friends). They'll download and review same-day. |
| +24h | Send email to broader list (colleagues, extended friends, professional network). |
| +48h | If reviews are landing, post a screenshot of the App Store listing on LinkedIn / Twitter with the founder story. |
| +1 week | Follow up individually with anyone who said "I'll do it" but hasn't. |
